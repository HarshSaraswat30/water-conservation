Use drip irrigation instead of sprinklers.

**Use Drip Irrigation Instead of Sprinklers** Drip irrigation is a highly efficient method of watering plants that delivers water directly to the roots through a network of tubes and emitters. Unlike traditional sprinklers that spray water over a wide area—often leading to evaporation, runoff, and water waste—drip systems minimize loss by targeting specific plants with slow, steady moisture. This method not only conserves water but also promotes healthier plant growth by avoiding overwatering and reducing the risk of fungal diseases. Especially in dry or drought-prone regions, switching to drip irrigation is a smart and sustainable choice for both home gardens and agricultural fields.

General Awareness

Information Regaurding Water Conservation

Freshwater is limited:

Only about 1% of Earth's water is easily accessible for human use—most of it is saltwater or trapped in glaciers.

Growing population, growing demand:

As global population increases, so does the demand for water, putting pressure on already strained resources.

Water and energy are connected:

Treating and transporting water requires energy—conserving water also reduces our carbon footprint.

Pollution makes water unusable:

Chemicals, plastics, and untreated sewage contaminate freshwater sources, reducing the amount of clean water available.

Small actions make a big impact:

Simple steps like turning off taps, using water-saving appliances, and collecting rainwater can significantly reduce usage.

Everyone has a role to play:

Governments, communities, schools, and individuals must work together to protect this precious resource.
